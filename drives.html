    DRIVES

    FOOD DISTRIBUTION DRIVE

    Starvation hit the impoverished due to pandemic. At this time helping hut organized food distribution drives to help the impoverished. Drives were organized in cities - Patna, New Delhi and Kota. Food packets were distributed in D block, Gali No. 5, Bharat , Vihar Kakrola by team New Delhi in collaboration with NSS, DDUC on 10th April,2021. ... Team Kota also reached the poverty stricken people in Village khariya, Rajasthan on 26 May,2021 and distributed raw food materials. 500+ food packets were distributed by Team Patna,New Delhi and Kota. Proper Covid Protocols were followed by our volunteers , every volunteer was masked ,carried their own Sanitizer, followed social distancing norms in each distribution drive.

    SANITARY PADS DISTRIBUTION DRIVE

    Menstruation is a normal and healthy part of life for most women. A lack of information about menstruation and 'taboos' that we have in society causes girls to miss out on normal childhood experiences and activities. The volunteers of Helping Hut spread awareness regarding the menstruation and ...importance of Sanitary pads usage instead of using dirty clothes, leaves , cotton and even sometimes ash . We distributed 500+ Sanitary pads in the slum areas to the women and adolescent girls. Many women even shared their experiences and troubles faced by them and appreciated our efforts which was visible through their relieved smiles .We hope to achieve much larger and better goals in future that can put smiles on the faces of people.

    CLOTHES DISTRIBUTION DRIVE

    There are many underprivileged, impoverished people who don't even have clothes to wear that are not torn off. We came across many such people who have been surviving in such poverty stricken conditions. To give an underprivileged person a pair of clothes is as good as dressing them with self respect. ... The Team Patna, Gwalior and Dwarka organised cloth distribution campaigns and distributed 500+clothes to the needy donated by our fellow volunteers and people to us. This effort of "The Helping Hut" endeavoured to bring some ray of hope in the lives of the needy.

    "Literacy in itself is no education. Literacy is not the end of education or even the beginning.     RATION DISTRIBUTION DRIVE

    "The unequal distribution of food is one of the world's most tragic facts. Millions of people die because they have too little to eat, and many die because they have too much". -Laura Huxley. Food is the most basic necessity of any organism to survive. According to EPA in 2018, the commercial, institutional and residential sectors created an estimate *63.1 million tonnes of food being wasted. ... When we think that this particular food item is waste instead of dumping it we can give it to the starving section of society if the food is under the proper conditions to be edible. The country is currently combating the second wave of Covid 19 due to an unexpected increase in food insecurity and demand for free food for the poor. At this time Team Dwarka and Team Kota of Helping Hut reached out to the most vulnerable sections of society, namely daily wage labourers and farmers and distributed dry ration to them.Dry ration like rice, cooking oil, pulses, wheat flour was distributed by the team Kota and Dwarka in Village Kharija, Rajasthan on 26 May,2021 and in slum area near Dwarka sec 14 metro station on 31 October, 2021 respectively. We believe that everyone must help the needy ones as "We rise by lifting others' '.

    MASK DISTRIBUTION DRIVE

    The sudden outbreak of Covid Pandemic sent the whole world into a whirlwind. Nationwide lockdown was imposed to break the chain of infection. Wearing a mask was made mandatory . "Mask will become symbol of a civilized society. If you want to protect yourselves and others from the covid infection the use of mask is important" ... addressed by PM Narendra Modi via radio programme Mann Ki Baat. So to ensure that the impoverished section of the society who can't afford these masks "The Helping Hut" came forward and distributed 500+ masks to the needy. We will stay safe if we focus on masking, self wellness, vaccination, and most importantly, our sense of community.
